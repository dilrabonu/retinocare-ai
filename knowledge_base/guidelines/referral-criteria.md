# Referral Criteria

Routine referral to an ophthalmologist is appropriate when screening
identifies Mild or Moderate diabetic retinopathy without other visual
symptoms, allowing scheduling within the standard follow-up window.

Urgent referral is warranted when screening identifies Severe or
Proliferative diabetic retinopathy, or when a patient reports sudden
vision changes, floaters, or vision loss regardless of the screening
result -- these symptoms can indicate a sight-threatening complication
requiring same-week evaluation.

Emergency referral (same-day) is warranted for sudden, painless vision
loss, which can indicate a retinal detachment or vitreous hemorrhage
requiring immediate specialist attention.

In all cases, screening results support clinical decision-making but do
not replace a comprehensive dilated eye examination by an eye care
professional.

Source: paraphrased from general ophthalmology screening literature (WHO / AAO).
