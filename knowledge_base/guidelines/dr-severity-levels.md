# Diabetic Retinopathy Severity Levels

Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is graded on a five-point scale based on the
extent of visible damage to the retina's blood vessels.

No DR means no visible abnormalities are present on examination.

Mild DR is characterized by the presence of microaneurysms only -- small
bulges in the tiny blood vessels of the retina.

Moderate DR involves more than just microaneurysms, including some
blocked blood vessels, but not yet meeting the criteria for severe disease.

Severe DR involves extensive blocked blood vessels, depriving areas of
the retina of blood supply, which signals the eye is at high risk of
progressing further.

Proliferative DR is the most advanced stage, marked by the growth of new,
abnormal blood vessels on the retina's surface -- a response to widespread
oxygen deprivation. These fragile new vessels carry a high risk of
bleeding and retinal detachment.

Source: paraphrased from general ophthalmology screening literature (WHO / AAO).
