# Recommended Screening Intervals

Follow-up screening frequency depends on the severity level found at the
most recent examination.

For patients with No DR, annual screening is generally recommended,
since diabetic retinopathy is a progressive condition.

For Mild DR, screening every 9 to 12 months is typically advised to
monitor for progression.

For Moderate DR, more frequent monitoring every 6 months is often
recommended given the elevated risk of progression to sight-threatening
stages.

For Severe DR, referral to a retina specialist within 1 month is
recommended, alongside closer monitoring.

For Proliferative DR, urgent referral within days to a retina specialist
is recommended, as timely treatment significantly reduces the risk of
severe vision loss.

Source: paraphrased from general ophthalmology screening literature (WHO / AAO).
